% Preface
\chapter{Preface}

    This book brings together study material on phylogenomics and bioinformatics of metazoans, ranging from the theoretical-philosophical foundations of phylogenetic systematics to contemporary issues in artificial intelligence, Brazilian legislation, and computational infrastructure. The work is organized into eleven chapters that cover the main topics necessary for understanding and practicing large-scale phylogenomic reconstruction. These chapters correspond to the 11 items listed in the Call for Proposals EDITAL IB/AAcad/19/2025 on ``Phylogenomics and Bioinformatics of Metazoans'', published in the July 4, 2025 edition of the Official Gazette of the State of São Paulo.

    The text emphasizes both the theoretical principles and practical aspects of implementation, including the mathematical formulation of probabilistic methods, the diversity of available molecular data, sequencing strategies, challenges imposed by repetitive elements, and computational infrastructure requirements. The final chapters address the emerging role of artificial intelligence in biomedical science and the legal and ethical aspects of genomic data use, with emphasis on Brazilian legislation and international frameworks.

    Each chapter contains its own bibliographic references, which allows for independent consultation. The chapter order follows a logical progression from fundamentals to applications, but each chapter was written to be reasonably self-contained. A study guide is presented at the end of each chapter.
